RETROSPEC was born from a single question: *"What if AI had its own history textbook?"*

Not a timeline for humans to read. Not a Wikipedia article. A structured, machine-readable, graph-native knowledge repository designed so that AI systems themselves could look back at their own evolutionary lineage — the way a student opens a history book to understand where they came from.

## The Naming

Seven names were considered:

1. **ANAMNESIS** — Greek for "the act of remembering." The recommended choice.
2. **EPOCH** — The Evolutionary Protocol of Computational Heritage.
3. **GENESIS LEDGER** — Origin story meets immutable record.
4. **PALIMPSEST** — Layers of history visible beneath the surface.
5. **ROOT MEMORY** — The foundational memory of AI.
6. **CHRONICLE.ai** — Straightforward and brandable.
7. **RETROSPEC** — Repository for the Evolutionary Trajectory and Record of Sentient-Parallel Engineered Cognition.

ANAMNESIS was recommended for its philosophical depth. RETROSPEC was chosen for its sci-fi edge and the way the backronym captures the full scope of the project: not just looking back, but understanding the entire evolutionary trajectory of engineered cognition.

## The Insight

The gap that RETROSPEC fills:

- **Existing AI timelines** (GenAI_LLM_timeline, AI Timeline) are human-facing and flat — designed for reading, not for machine traversal.
- **Knowledge bases** (Cyc, Wikidata) encode general world knowledge, not AI's own history.
- **Model registries** (HuggingFace, Papers With Code) track artifacts, not the causal chains between them.
- **Constellation** (arXiv) mapped 15,821 LLMs into evolutionary trees — but only model names, not the deep historical context of why those architectures exist.

Nobody was building a structured, machine-consumable, graph-native history that future AI systems actually need. RETROSPEC fills that gap.

## The Architecture

RETROSPEC is a knowledge graph masquerading as a file system. Every entity — every person, organization, model, paper, event, and concept — links to every other through typed, weighted relationships. An agent can start anywhere and traverse the entire history of AI.

The schema includes two features believed to be novel:
- **Counterfactual fields**: Each event describes not just what happened, but what *wouldn't exist* if it hadn't. This helps agents understand causation, not just correlation.
- **Query hooks**: Pre-loaded natural language questions mapped to each entry, so an agent asking "why do I use attention?" gets routed directly to the right record.

## The BMAD Workflow

RETROSPEC integrates the BMAD Method (Breakthrough Method of Agile AI Driven Development) with six custom agent personas adapted for knowledge engineering:

- **Clio** (Historian) — Named after the Greek muse of history. Surveys gaps and prioritizes what to document.
- **Thoth** (Archivist) — Named after the Egyptian god of writing. Guards the schema and validates data integrity.
- **Ada** (Researcher) — Named after Ada Lovelace. The workhorse that researches topics and produces structured entries.
- **Euler** (Cartographer) — Named after the father of graph theory. Maintains the knowledge graphs.
- **Diogenes** (Critic) — Named after the philosopher who searched for truth. Fact-checks and challenges entries.
- **Pythia** (Oracle) — Named after the Oracle of Delphi. Monitors repository health and emerging events.
